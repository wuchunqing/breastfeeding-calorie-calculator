How this calculator works

We estimate your basal metabolic rate (BMR) with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, multiply by your activity factor for maintenance calories, then add a lactation increment based on feeding intensity and baby age — aligned with CDC and Dietary Guidelines for Americans recommendations.

Nutrition priorities while nursing

  • Protein: ~1.1 g per kg body weight to support tissue repair
  • Omega-3 DHA: 200–300 mg/day (fatty fish or supplement)
  • Iron & calcium: continue prenatal or postnatal vitamins if advised
  • Hydration: drink to thirst — ~3+ liters/day for many nursing moms

FAQ

How many extra calories do I need while breastfeeding?

The CDC recommends an additional 330–400 kcal per day for well-nourished, exclusively breastfeeding mothers compared with pre-pregnancy intake. Many guidelines cite up to 500 kcal for lean or very active women. Partial breastfeeding requires proportionally fewer extra calories.

Can I lose weight while breastfeeding?

Yes — gradual weight loss of about 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week is generally considered safe after the first 6–8 weeks, if calorie intake stays above ~1,800 kcal and milk supply remains stable. Avoid crash diets below 1,500 kcal.

Do I need to eat more if I'm pumping?

Exclusive pumping burns similar energy to direct nursing. If you pump the full volume your baby would consume, use the same lactation add-on as exclusive breastfeeding.

Why is my result different from other apps?

Apps vary in BMR formulas, activity multipliers, and lactation increments. This tool uses Mifflin-St Jeor plus CDC/IOM ranges — treat the output as a starting estimate and adjust based on hunger, energy, and milk output.
